The Seam Tax

Every tool flags a problem and hands the work back

Each tool is a database with a UI bolted on. Individually each is a partial answer — and combined, they are a worse answer, because the value dies at every seam. The integration tax is the product.

What the money actually buys

  • CarePort / PCC Referral Advisor summarize the packet — then a human re-keys it
  • Availity / Waystar / Experian verify & predict — then a human reads a screen and acts
  • Zimmet / SimpleAnalyzer flag MDS risk — then the coordinator codes and submits
  • Inovalon scrubs claims into a worklist — then a biller clears edits one by one
  • BPOs (Richter, LTC Ally) are people-as-software, billed at 4–7% of collections
  • “Autonomous” RCM startups are RPA bots with a human escalation queue

Where the value dies

  • Referral network doesn’t write to the EHR → three re-keyings
  • Therapy is a separate vendor → GG scores reconciled by eye
  • Clearinghouse never tells the EHR why a claim died → no feedback loop
  • Contract isn’t in the billing system → underpayments silently written off
  • Nobody closes the loop — the submit/appeal/recover step is always rules-engine-plus-human
  • The combined stack isn’t a platform — it’s 8 silos and a human running between them

None of this is fixable by adding a ninth tool. Every failure is a seam failure.

The Silent Leak

The $15K–$80K a month
you write off every month

MA per-diem underpayment is the single largest unaddressed SNF revenue leak — and it is invisible because the contract lives in a PDF, not in the billing system. The remit comes in low, and the difference is booked as “contractual.”

Why no vendor catches it

Horizontal RCM players (Waystar / FinThrive / R1) have the AI muscle — but their contract engines model hospital DRG / case-rate logic, not SNF per-diem. SNF-native tools (SNF Metrics) make you hand-enter contracts and only track — they don’t auto-compare line-level remits. Neither cluster covers per-diem-vs-remit detection wired natively to the EHR.

What Aedis does instead

Loads every contract’s per-diem rate logic and compares the contracted per-diem against the actually-remitted per-diem at the 835 line — surfacing systemic per-payer patterns and arming renegotiation with hard data. This is the named industry white-space, and it only exists if one agent reads census + claim + remit on one graph.
