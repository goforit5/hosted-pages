Section 01

Platform Intelligence

The command center that replaces weekly reports, "management by walking around," and the 47 spreadsheets your regional directors maintain.

Enterprise-Wide Real-Time Dashboard

  • 330+ facility status — every facility's operational health rendered in a single view. Census, staffing ratios, clinical alerts, financial performance, compliance scores. Updated continuously, not weekly.
  • Exception-based management — agents surface only what needs human judgment. If 328 facilities are operating normally, you see the 2 that aren't. No noise. Your administrators get the same view scoped to their facility. Your regional directors see their region. You see everything.
  • Cross-facility pattern detection — what's happening at Heritage Oaks today happened at Meadowbrook 6 months ago. Same staffing pattern. Same census trajectory. Same supplier issues. The platform sees it because it has memory across 330+ facilities. No human can hold that context.
  • Morning briefing generation — replaces the DON's 6 AM email chain. By the time your operators open their laptops, a prioritized briefing is waiting: overnight incidents, staffing gaps for the day, admissions pipeline, items requiring immediate attention, items agents already handled.

Decision Queue & Governance

  • Decision queue — a prioritized list of items needing human approval. Each item includes the agent's recommendation, confidence score, supporting evidence, and policy references. Approve, reject, or escalate in under 10 seconds. No digging through records to understand context.
  • Governance levels — six levels, from fully autonomous to human-required. Invoice processing under $500? Autonomous. Vendor contract renewal? Human approval. Employee termination? Multiple sign-offs. Large capital purchases? CFO + CEO. Every organization defines its own thresholds.
  • Kill switches — any agent can be paused instantly at the facility, region, or enterprise level. If a new CMS rule changes the compliance landscape overnight, pause the compliance agents, update the rules, resume. No code deployments. No downtime for other agents.
  • Role-based views — CEO sees enterprise. CFO sees financial. DON sees clinical. Administrator sees their facility. Same platform, same data, different lens. No one sees data they shouldn't. RBAC enforced at every layer.

Audit Trail

  • Every agent action logged — full evidence chain. What was detected, what data was analyzed, what decision was made, what policy was referenced, what action was taken. Immutable. Timestamped. Attributable.
  • Agent activity monitoring — which agents ran, what they processed, what they found, how long it took. Performance dashboards for your AI workforce, just like you monitor your human workforce.
  • Decision replay — go back to any decision any agent made and understand exactly why. Not "the AI decided" — but "the agent identified a 12% variance in dietary supply costs at Facility #247, compared it against the contracted rate in the GPO agreement dated March 2025, and flagged it as a potential overbilling event with 94% confidence."
What This Replaces

Weekly reports that are already stale by Tuesday. Phone calls between facilities and corporate asking "what's the census today?" Regional directors maintaining 47 spreadsheets. Management by walking around. The monthly board report that took 3 people 2 weeks to compile. The DON's 6 AM email chain that no one reads past the third paragraph.

Section 02

Clinical Operations

Clinical is your core business. This is the largest section because it covers the most ground. Every resident. Every facility. Every shift. Continuously.

Continuous Resident Monitoring

  • 24/7 clinical status monitoring — every resident's vitals, ADL status, medications, incidents, and care plan adherence tracked across all facilities. Not sampled. Not audited quarterly. Continuous. The agent reads PCC data as it's entered and acts on it.
  • Fall risk detection — pattern analysis across medications (sedatives, antihypertensives, psychotropics), cognition scores, mobility status, time of day, staffing levels, and facility layout. High-risk residents flagged with specific intervention recommendations before the fall happens.
  • Fall cascade protocol — 3rd fall in 30 days triggers automatic care conference scheduling, family notification draft, physician review request, care plan update initiation, F-tag risk assessment, and incident trending report. The DON doesn't chase this — the agent orchestrates it and puts approvals in the decision queue.
  • Weight loss surveillance — greater than 5% in 30 days or 10% in 180 days triggers dietary consult, lab panel order, physician notification, and nutritional supplement review. CMS requirement. Most facilities catch this late. The agent catches it the day it crosses the threshold.

Wound, Infection & Medication Management

  • Wound management — staging accuracy verification, measurement tracking against healing trajectory, treatment protocol compliance, turning/repositioning schedule adherence, and Braden Scale reassessment triggers. A stage 3 pressure ulcer that isn't healing on expected trajectory gets flagged to the wound care nurse and physician within hours, not weeks.
  • Infection surveillance — UTI tracking (the #1 infection in SNFs), wound infections, respiratory infections, antibiotic stewardship metrics, culture/sensitivity results, and outbreak detection. When 3 residents on the same unit develop UTIs in the same week, the agent triggers an infection control investigation before it becomes an outbreak.
  • Medication reconciliation — cross-checking orders against diagnoses, Beers Criteria for elderly patients, drug interaction screening, duplicate therapy detection, therapeutic appropriateness, and dose adjustments for renal/hepatic function. Every admission, every transfer, every physician visit.
  • Psychotropic medication monitoring — PRN usage frequency tracking, gradual dose reduction (GDR) compliance, behavioral documentation to support continued use, non-pharmacological intervention documentation, and CMS F-758 compliance. This is survey gold. Most facilities get tagged here. Yours won't.
  • Pain management — pain assessment compliance (frequency, tool consistency), intervention effectiveness tracking, non-pharmacological alternative documentation, PRN pain medication utilization patterns, and reassessment after intervention.
  • Lab result monitoring — critical value alerts routed to nursing and physician simultaneously, trending for chronic conditions (A1c for diabetics, INR for anticoagulation, renal function panels, thyroid levels), and automatic lab reorder scheduling based on physician-defined intervals.

Assessments & Documentation

  • MDS assessment tracking — quarterly assessments, annual assessments, OBRA triggers, significant change assessments, significant correction assessments, and Medicare-required assessments. Due dates tracked automatically. MDS coordinators get a daily queue, not a spreadsheet to maintain. ARD windows optimized for maximum reimbursement.
  • Care plan timeliness — updates within 48 hours of any significant change (CMS requirement). The agent monitors for triggers: new diagnosis, change in functional status, new medication, fall, hospitalization, weight change, behavior change. Generates care plan update drafts with relevant clinical data pre-populated.
  • Physician order management — verbal order co-signature tracking (must be signed within state-specific timeframes), telephone order verification, order reconciliation at admission/transfer/discharge, and PRN effectiveness documentation.
  • Documentation exceptions — unsigned orders, late entries, missing assessments, incomplete care plans, unsigned progress notes, missing physician certifications/recertifications. Flagged before they become survey findings. The agent doesn't nag — it routes to the right person with the right context at the right time.

Therapy, Nutrition & Support Services

  • Therapy services — PT/OT/ST session tracking, functional gains documentation, Medicare minute thresholds, authorization tracking, concurrent/group therapy documentation, discharge planning, and carryover program documentation. Section GG scoring accuracy directly impacts PDPM reimbursement — the agent ensures documentation supports the clinical picture.
  • Dietary/nutrition — meal intake monitoring (less than 75% intake triggers intervention), supplement compliance, therapeutic diet adherence, food allergy management, dining assistance needs, hydration tracking, and weight monitoring integration.
  • Social services — advance directive completion tracking, discharge planning initiation at admission, family meeting coordination and documentation, behavioral health screening, mood assessment (PHQ-9) tracking, and resident rights compliance.
  • Readmission risk — hospital readmission prediction using clinical indicators (polypharmacy, recent ED visits, unstable conditions, functional decline), transition of care protocol compliance, 30-day readmission tracking by facility, and root cause analysis for readmissions. Readmissions cost money and tank your quality measures.
What This Replaces

Manual chart reviews that take the DON 4 hours daily. Nurses doing paper audits on nights and weekends. MDS coordinators manually tracking assessment due dates in spreadsheets. Wound care nurses keeping paper measurement logs. Infection preventionists doing monthly chart pulls. The clinical team spending their time auditing instead of caring for residents.

Section 03

Revenue Cycle

Your AP team processes 200 invoices a day. Our agents process 2,000 — with higher accuracy, better GL coding, and zero duplicate payments.

Billing & Claims

  • Claims submission — automated coding from clinical documentation, clean claim rate optimization, real-time eligibility verification, and pre-submission validation against payer-specific rules. The agent reads the clinical record, maps diagnoses to billing codes, verifies coverage, and submits. Your billers review exceptions, not every claim.
  • Denial management — automatic appeal generation for common denial reasons (medical necessity, timely filing, authorization), pattern analysis on denials by payer/facility/denial reason, root cause identification, and appeal success rate tracking. When Humana starts denying a specific code at a higher rate, the agent detects the pattern and adjusts pre-submission validation.
  • AR aging management — automated follow-up sequences at 30/60/90/120 days, escalation routing, payer-specific follow-up protocols, write-off recommendations with supporting documentation, and bad debt analysis. The agent doesn't let claims age — it follows up before you would.
  • PDPM optimization — MDS-driven reimbursement maximization. Section GG scoring accuracy audit, therapy minute threshold monitoring, nursing component classification verification, NTA component validation, and SLP component documentation. The difference between accurate and inaccurate PDPM classification across 330 facilities is tens of millions annually.
  • Managed care contract management — rate comparison across contracts, utilization tracking against contracted volumes, authorization management (initial and concurrent), timely filing deadline monitoring, and contract renewal analysis with rate benchmarking.
  • Medicare Part A/B billing — benefit period tracking, SNF-level billing, therapy billing compliance (Part B), ESRD billing, and ancillary services billing. Medicaid billing: state-specific requirements across all 17 states, level of care documentation, rate updates, and spend-down tracking.

Accounts Payable

  • Invoice ingestion — invoices arrive via email, fax, vendor portals, and mail. The agent ingests from all sources, extracts line items using document intelligence, and creates payable records. No manual data entry. No keying errors.
  • 3-way matching — automatic matching of purchase order, goods receipt, and invoice. Price variance detection, quantity variance detection, and GL coding based on PO category and historical patterns. Match rate targets: 85%+ straight-through processing.
  • Invoice exception handling — price variances beyond tolerance, unknown vendors, closed project codes, budget threshold violations, missing PO references, duplicate invoices, and payment term discrepancies. Each exception routed to the right approver with full context.
  • Vendor management — COI (Certificate of Insurance) tracking and expiration alerts, W-9 currency verification, sanctions screening (OIG, SAM, state exclusion lists), contract compliance monitoring, payment term optimization, and early payment discount capture.
  • Contract pricing enforcement — automatic comparison of invoiced prices vs contracted rates for every line item. When a supplier invoices at $4.50/unit and your GPO contract says $3.80, the agent flags it before payment. Across 330 facilities with thousands of vendors, this alone pays for the platform.

Payroll & Month-End

  • Payroll processing — 892 timecards audited per day across your facilities. Overtime detection and cost allocation, meal break compliance verification (state-specific rules across 17 states), credential verification against scheduled position requirements, and differential/premium pay calculation.
  • Payroll exceptions — missed punches, rate mismatches, duplicate shifts, agency labor reconciliation (agency invoiced hours vs facility-recorded hours), unauthorized overtime, and schedule vs actual variance.
  • Monthly close — automated checklist execution: bank reconciliations, subledger reconciliations, accrual calculations, depreciation runs, intercompany eliminations, and variance commentary generation. Your controllers get a pre-populated close package, not a blank checklist. Close timeline compressed from 15 days to 5.
  • Budget vs actual — real-time variance monitoring by facility, department, and cost center. Trend analysis with forward projections. Automatic reforecasting based on census and acuity trends. Variance commentary drafted for material items.
  • Treasury/cash management — daily cash position monitoring, covenant tracking against credit agreements, banking relationship management, and cash flow forecasting based on AR/AP pipelines.
  • Purchase order management — requisition workflow automation, approval routing based on amount/category/facility, budget checking at requisition time (not after the money's spent), and PO-to-invoice lifecycle tracking.
What This Replaces

Billing staff manually coding claims from clinical records. AP clerks keying invoices by hand. Controllers running month-end close from a 47-item checklist. Payroll staff auditing timecards one by one. CFO getting financial reports 2 weeks after month-end. AR staff manually calling payers for follow-up. The entire "print, review, approve, scan" paper workflow.

  • Agency labor management — agency usage monitoring by facility/unit/shift, cost comparison vs overtime vs float pool, preferred agency routing based on performance and rate, and invoice reconciliation (agency billed hours vs facility recorded hours).   • Training compliance — required training tracking: OSHA (bloodborne pathogens, hazard communication), HIPAA, abuse prevention and reporting, fire safety, infection control, dementia care (state-specific requirements), and role-specific competencies. Automatically assigns training, tracks completion, and generates compliance reports.
  • Annual in-service management — tracking completion across 330+ facilities for mandatory annual training. Generating compliance reports for survey readiness. Identifying facilities below threshold with time remaining.

Employee Lifecycle

  • Employee relations — investigation tracking with timeline management, disciplinary action documentation and consistency analysis (did similar situations get similar outcomes across facilities?), and grievance resolution tracking.
  • Performance management — evaluation due date tracking, performance improvement plan management, goal tracking, and 90-day review compliance for new hires.
  • Benefits administration — enrollment tracking, cost analysis, utilization monitoring, and open enrollment management across the enterprise.
  • Workers compensation — claim tracking, OSHA 300 log management, return-to-work program coordination, experience modification rate tracking, and safety incident trending by facility/department/role.
  • Retention analytics — turnover by facility/role/tenure/shift, exit interview analysis with theme detection, stay interview triggers (when a high-performer shows flight risk indicators), and compensation competitiveness analysis. The agent identifies which facilities are bleeding nurses 6 months before the turnover spike hits.

  • Payer mix optimization — target vs actual payer mix by facility, impact analysis on revenue per patient day, and admission screening recommendations based on payer mix goals. When a facility is heavy on Medicaid, the agent prioritizes Medicare and managed care referrals.

  • Marketing/business development — campaign tracking, community outreach activity logging, physician liaison visit scheduling and outcome tracking, and competitive intelligence on new facilities or closures in the market.
