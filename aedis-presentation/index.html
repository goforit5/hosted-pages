Vendor Analysis

Your vendors are adding AI — shaped by their priorities

PCC — Embedded AI in Clinical Workflows

PCC is adding AI inside its own interface — note extraction, documentation suggestions, risk scoring. These are useful within their scope, but they operate only within PCC's environment, on PCC data, through PCC's UI. They don't connect to financial, HR, or compliance systems, and they're not designed around Ensign's specific operating model across 330+ facilities.

Workday — AI as a Control Plane for Digital Labor

Workday is positioning as the orchestration layer for AI agents — identity, audit, agent management. Architecturally sound within their ecosystem, but it means Ensign's AI capabilities become bounded by what Workday chooses to expose. The agents operate within Workday's platform model rather than across Ensign's full operational landscape.

Both add value within their platforms. The opportunity for Ensign is owning the orchestration layer that connects all systems to its specific workflows, controls, and priorities.

Security Architecture

PHI is tokenized at the boundary —
agents never see raw names, MRNs, or DOBs

Six trust zones. Raw PHI lives in a vault no agent can reach. OCR and vision run inside the HIPAA boundary (BAA-covered Textract + Bedrock vision in-VPC). Only tokenized output crosses to the agent workspace. Rehydration to cleartext requires a human under RBAC + purpose-of-use + MFA — and is logged on every read.

What the agent sees

Tokens only. resident_tok_7J4K9Q, mrn_tok_B8Z1P2, dob_tok_3FD9A1. The agent has no tool path to display_mrn, read_phi_vault, or decrypt_raw_document — those tools are not registered in the agent's session.

What we do not claim

We do not claim de-identification under HIPAA Safe Harbor or Expert Determination. We use tokenization as our operational privacy control — defensible, auditable, least-privilege, BAA-covered. De-identification is a separate compliance determination we have not pursued.

Phase 1: Anthropic Managed Agents under BAA, tokenization at the boundary. Phase 2: Bedrock-Managed-Agents in Ensign's VPC — PHI never leaves your cloud.

Engineering Concerns

Three objections your team should raise —
and the architecture that answers them

Hallucination in Production

LLMs can generate plausible-but-wrong output. That is exactly why the system uses retrieval, grounding, validation, and structured proposals. Every agent action produces cited sources, confidence scores, and diff previews. Humans approve. Wrong outputs are caught before they execute.

AI as Chatbot, Not Systems Component

Most enterprise AI is a chat window bolted onto existing software. Our agents are bounded processes: they read from APIs, execute defined workflows, and surface structured decisions. No chat interface. Structured input, structured output, governed execution. Schema enforcement, retries, fallbacks, confidence thresholds.

Agentic Means Autonomous Black Box

We are not proposing unsupervised autonomous decision-making. Agents autonomously gather, synthesize, triage, draft, and recommend. Humans remain accountable for material decisions. Every agent action is logged with timestamp, data accessed, reasoning, and outcome. More auditable than manual work.

These are legitimate concerns. The architecture is designed specifically to address them.

Governance & Control

Governed AI — from data access
to production deployment

Every agent action is auditable, reversible, and human-approved. Autonomous preparation with human accountability preserved.

Human-in-the-Loop

Agents propose, humans approve. Every PCC write-back shows a diff preview. No autonomous writes without explicit approval.

Complete Audit Trail

Every action logged — timestamp, user, data accessed, rationale. Three-layer audit: hooks, middleware, DB triggers. SOX-ready from day one.

Role-Based Access

Agents inherit user permissions. Nurse sees her unit. DON sees her facility. Executive sees the enterprise. Same RBAC you already enforce.

Regulatory Compliance

  • HIPAA — BAA-covered, PHI never leaves your VPC
  • SOX — Immutable logs, separation of duties, approvals
  • CMS — F-tag mapping, survey readiness, reg monitoring
  • State regs — Multi-state rules built into agent logic

AI Safety Controls

  • Hallucination guard — Sources cited, confidence scored
  • Read-only default — Write requires explicit approval
  • Governance levels — Full approval → relax as trust earned
  • Kill switch — Disable any agent instantly, per-facility

Reliability & Consistency

Agents run defined workflows, not open-ended generation. Same input produces same structured output. Monitored, versioned, and regression-tested like any production system.

Adoption & Change Management

Agents prepare decisions. Humans approve in under 10 seconds. No training on new software. No workflow disruption. The interface is a decision queue, not an application.

Data Privacy & Governance

PHI processed in-VPC. Role-based data access inherited from existing RBAC. No data leaves Ensign's cloud boundary. Every data touch audited end-to-end.
