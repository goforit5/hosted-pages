The Overhead Today

What the billing office actually costs

Three layers stacked on top of each other: people, licenses, and leakage. Every one of them is a database with a UI bolted on — and every one of them flags a problem, then hands the work back to a human.

The people layer

Roughly 1 biller per 100–150 beds, plus shared MDS-coordinator and business-office-manager time. MDS coordinators alone lose 2+ hours every day to manual chart abstraction — the #1 driver of both under-capture and clawback.

The license layer

PCC EHR + a clearinghouse (Waystar/Availity) + a PDPM optimizer (Zimmet/SimpleAnalyzer) + an eligibility/auth point tool + an outsourced CBO at 4–7% of collections. Per-claim transaction fees layered on top of loaded biller labor.

The leakage layer

A 12–15% denial rate (4–5× the <3% target), only ~18% of overturnable MA denials ever appealed, and $15K–$80K per facility per month in silent per-diem underpayment booked as “contractual.” Days-in-AR running past 45–50.

The tools read the data. Everything between them is people — and everything they miss is margin.

The Largest Single Line

The $15K–$80K a month no vendor catches

Horizontal RCM players have the AI muscle — but their contract engines model hospital DRG/case-rate logic, not SNF per-diem. SNF-native tools make you hand-enter contracts and only track them. Neither cluster auto-compares the contracted per-diem against the actually-remitted per-diem at the 835 line, wired to the EHR. That is the named industry white-space.
