The Agentic Inversion

Same data. Opposite design.

A SNF’s revenue cycle isn’t a judgment problem — it’s a labor problem disguised as complexity. The work is knowable from the chart, the contract, the clock, and payer history. The market loses by default not because the work is hard, but because doing it is manual, deadline-driven, and prohibitively staffed.

Incumbent One verb, then the human

  • Clearinghouses submit faster — a biller still clears every edit
  • Scrubbers score denial risk — a coordinator still codes and sends
  • Legal copilots draft a letter — and can’t even read the chart
  • Contract tools store per-diems — they tell you you were underpaid later
  • Auth bots transmit a 278 — the nurse already spent 45 min deciding the LOC
  • BPOs sell labor by the hour at 4–7% of collections
  • Payer UM tools (naviHealth, Cohere) are built to deny the provider

Aedis The agent does the work

  • Reads the PointClickCare chart and determines the level of care itself
  • Root-causes a denial to the exact MDS element — then patches the upstream rule
  • Pulls the complete PCC/Workday/M365 record and builds the chronology
  • Compares contracted vs remitted per-diem at the 835 line and recovers it
  • Negotiates a payment plan inside a hard policy envelope, then executes
  • Owns the whole referral→cash relay — no human anchor leg
  • Provider-side counterweight: pre-builds the proof and the appeal before the denial

Even fully stacked, the incumbents are a relay race with a human running the anchor leg between every product. Aedis runs the whole race.
